# Our Thesis: The Future of Discoverability

*Living document. Last updated: March 2, 2026.*

## The Big Picture

Discovery is shifting from **human-driven search** to **agent-mediated selection**.

Today: A human googles "best CRM for startups," reads 10 blog posts, watches 3 YouTube videos, asks friends on Twitter, and picks one.

Tomorrow: An agent evaluates 200 CRMs in seconds based on the user's actual needs, budget, tech stack, and usage patterns — and picks one. Or three. Or builds a custom workflow combining pieces of five.

**This changes everything about how companies get found.**

## Key Shifts

### 1. From Eyeballs to Evaluations
- Old world: Get in front of humans (ads, SEO, content marketing)
- New world: Pass agent evaluation criteria (structured data, API quality, reliability scores)
- The "customer" making the choice is increasingly an AI, not a human

### 2. From Keywords to Capabilities
- Old world: Rank for "best project management tool"
- New world: Be the tool that agents select when a user says "help me manage my team's sprint"
- Capability matching > keyword matching

### 3. From Brands to Benchmarks
- Old world: Brand recognition drives choice ("I've heard of Salesforce")
- New world: Performance data drives choice ("This tool has 99.9% uptime and handles your exact use case")
- Agents don't have brand loyalty. They have performance data.

### 4. From Pages to Protocols
- Old world: Your website is your storefront
- New world: Your API/MCP schema is your storefront
- If an agent can't programmatically understand what you do, you don't exist

### 5. From Marketing to Machine-Readability
- Old world: Write compelling copy for humans
- New world: Write compelling metadata for agents AND humans
- Both matter, but the balance is shifting

## The Discoverability Stack (Our Framework)

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│  Layer 4: INTENT MATCHING           │  Does the agent choose you when
│  (Prompt/Organic Discovery)         │  someone has the right intent?
├─────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Layer 3: EVALUATION                │  When compared, do you win?
│  (Benchmarks, reliability, reviews) │  Performance, cost, capabilities.
├─────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Layer 2: VISIBILITY                │  Can agents find you at all?
│  (App stores, registries, indexes)  │  Listings, categories, rankings.
├─────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Layer 1: MACHINE-READABILITY       │  Can agents understand you?
│  (APIs, MCP, schemas, docs)         │  Structured data, protocols.
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

Most companies are stuck at Layer 1-2. The winners will own Layer 3-4.

## Open Questions

- How will agent "trust" develop? Will there be agent-to-agent reputation systems?
- Will there be an "advertising" model for agents? Pay-per-selection?
- How does this interact with regulations? (EU AI Act, etc.)
- What happens to brand marketing when agents don't care about brands?
- Will agents develop preferences/biases? How do you detect and optimize for them?
- How do multi-agent systems (where agents recommend agents) change the game?
- What's the role of human override? ("I don't care what the agent says, I want Notion")

## What We Believe (Strong Opinions, Loosely Held)

1. **Structured data wins.** Companies that invest in machine-readable descriptions of their capabilities will dominate.
2. **Reliability is the new brand.** Agents will track uptime, error rates, response times. This becomes your reputation.
3. **The app store model is transitional.** Browsable directories are a stepping stone. The end state is agents that just know what's available.
4. **AEO (AI Engine Optimization) will be bigger than SEO.** And it's happening now, not in 5 years.
5. **Connectors are the new distribution.** Being inside Claude Cowork, ChatGPT, etc. = being on the shelf at Walmart.
6. **The platform that helps companies navigate this shift will be massive.** That's what we're building.
